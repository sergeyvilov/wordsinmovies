The word frequency lists available here contain important information about word statistics (frequency and dispersion) in contemporary spoken language.

For each language, the wordlists are generated by selecting randomly a large number of subtitles files from the monolingual part of the OpenSubtitles corpus 2018.

Here, we present lemmatized lists, where all words sharing the same base form (so-called lemma) are represented by a single row.

To illustrate the concept of lemmatized lists, let us consider the verb "to make". All its forms, e.g. "making", "made", "makes", will be attributed to the same row with the lemma "make" and labelled with the part-of-speech (POS) tag "VB" (verb).

Our lemmatized lists contain the number of occurences of each lemma in the analyzed part of the corpus (word frequency). In addition, for each lemma we calculate a dispersion metric which shows how a given word is spread across the corpus. As a dispersion metric, we use the Jullian's D parameter (Jullian, 1970) with the correction on the size of each subtitles file with respect to the analyzed corpus volume (Gries, 2008). We also compute the rank of each word as a product between the word frequency and the Jullian's D parameter. Computed in this way, the rank can be considered as a measure of importance of each word in speech.

Our wordlists can be used, for example, to

  1. Choose priority words for studying
  2. Look for new words (they are at the end of the list!)
  3. Learn about relative importance of different issues in life (which word is ranked higher - "war" or "love" ?)

To learn how the databases were analyzed, view the technical note.

Definitions of different POS tags can be found here.
POS tags for nouns, proper nouns, verbs, adjectives and adverbs are grouped according to the following rules:

(NN, NNS) --> NN
(NNP, NNPS) --> NP
(VBD, VBG, VBN, VBP, VBZ) --> VB
(JJ, JJR, JJS) --> JJ
(RB, RBR, RBS) --> RB

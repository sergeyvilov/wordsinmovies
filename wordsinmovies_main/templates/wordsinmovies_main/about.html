The site

The WordsInMovies website provides a connection beetween the large collection of multilingual movie subtitles ( OpenSubtitles corpus ) and language learners, teachers and specialists in linguistics. In the core of the WordsInMovies website is a search function that permits to look for a particular word/phrase in context and its translation to a given language. Most of the words are labelled with the part-of-speech(POS) tag to enable fine searching.

The main objective is to show how the translation (and the meaning!) of a given word varies depending on the situational context.

Language learners, teachers and experts in linguistics can benefit from the WordsInMovies website in a number of ways:
  • Language learners can expand their vocabulary knowledge by observing how the target word is used in different situations. Students may find examples from movie subtitles more attractive compared to classbook exercises that sometimes look unrealistic. A translation to the student mother tongue is intended to facilitate the mastery of a given word/phrase on one hand and make the student aware of translation pitfalls on the other hand. The link to the movie imdb page available under each search result will make the activity even more engaging.
  • Language teachers can also expand their vocabulary knowledge using this website. In addition, they may use the site to look for sentences examples to use in the classroom.
  • Experts in linguistics may mostly be interested in the word frequency lists . They can also use the website as a source of examples for language textbooks.

The website supports a variety of search options, including POS-based and wildcard-based search. Unregistered users can make up to 50 queries per day, for registered users the number of queries is limited by 100.

The author

The WordsInMovies website was entirely created by Sergey Vilov, who analysed large subtitles databases of the OpenSubtitles corpus 2018, performed POS-tagging, implemented full-text search and designed the front-end and back-end of this site.
